Master the science of cognitive signatures.

Program Overview

Professional certification in a new discipline.

CNAP — Certified NOST Analysis Practitioner — is the professional certification for clinicians who want to integrate Beverly Index technology into their practice. The program covers the full scientific foundation, technology ecosystem, clinical application, and ethical framework of Computational Psychodynamics.

Graduates earn the CNAP credential, are listed in the public practitioner directory, and gain full access to the NOUS platform's CI (Clinical Intelligence) pathway with ongoing support and continuing education.

The program is designed for licensed mental health professionals — psychologists, psychiatrists, clinical social workers, marriage and family therapists, professional counselors, and psychiatric nurse practitioners — though we welcome applications from other qualified healthcare providers.

Curriculum

Six modules. Complete mastery.

Module 1

Foundations of NOST

Neural Oscillation Signature Theory — the three premises, five frequency bands, oscillatory specificity, and the scientific evidence base. Why language carries neural signatures.

Module 2

NOSS Classification System

All 12 NOSS sectors, integration patterns (NORM-I through DISS-T), sector activation thresholds, multi-label classification, and clinical interpretation frameworks.

Module 3

NOUS Platform Mastery

Hands-on training with the NOUS platform — AAE intake, LOGOS calibration, BLUF dashboard navigation, Beverly Index scoring interpretation, and report generation.

Module 4

Clinical Application

Assessment protocols for CI pathway — session design, narrative capture best practices, multi-session tracking, treatment intelligence integration, and patient communication.

Module 5

Output Interpretation

Reading DreamWeaver visualizations, Memoria Sonata compositions, and Empyrean 3D topologies. Translating multi-modal output into clinical insight and patient education.

Module 6

Ethics & Practice

Ethical framework for Computational Psychodynamics — informed consent, HIPAA compliance, scope of practice, cultural sensitivity with LOGOS, and responsible AI use in clinical settings.
