Five Key Findings

Finding 1: The Beverly Index Separates the Groups

Presidents: 58–66. Mass murderers: 72–100. No overlap. The platform produces a categorically different cognitive health reading for individuals with documented psychopathology versus neurotypical political rhetoric.

Finding 2: Integration Shifts from STUCK to HYPER

All three presidents were STUCK — language that identifies problems but doesn't resolve. Kaczynski and Breivik are both HYPER — language that moves rapidly across topics with elevated energy, starting and abandoning integration attempts. This is the linguistic signature of a mind that can't stop.

Finding 3: The Personality Sector Explodes

Presidents: 6.5–11.1%. Kaczynski: 42.8%. Breivik: 43.4%. The single largest sector divergence in the entire dataset. NOUS detected identity rigidity, black-and-white framing, and self-referential intensity at levels completely absent from political rhetoric.

Finding 4: Optimal Collapses

Presidents: 14–34%. All three mass murderers: 7–11%. The language of executive confidence, goal-directed reasoning, and adaptive problem-solving virtually disappears.

Finding 5: The Psychotic Sector Is Surprisingly Low

This is the most scientifically interesting finding. Kaczynski 8.8%, Breivik 8.1%, Rodger 19.8%. All three have documented psychiatric histories involving psychotic features, yet the Psychotic sector didn't dramatically elevate. This is because the Psychotic sector measures thought disorder — and all three manifestos are superficially coherent. The pathology shows up in Personality, Captivity, and the HYPER pattern, not in Psychotic. This distinction — between what someone thinks and how they organize their thinking — is exactly the kind of nuance a crude sentiment analysis tool would miss entirely.

This is discriminant validity. The instrument measures what it claims to measure. And the measurement works.
